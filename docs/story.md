# Story

## Premise

Mike is a laboratory test subject held inside a secret underground research facility. He has endured repeated and unethical experiments at the hands of cruel scientists who see him as material, not a person.

Then the system fails in the most embarrassing possible way: an unpaid intern makes a mistake, security breaks down, and Mike gets loose.

He does not get far before a guard opens fire. Mike drops, apparently finished.

Except he is not finished.

Instead of dying, he divides.

One Mike becomes two.

He gets shot again, and now there are three.

Mike realizes something important before anyone else does: he is invincible, but not in the way anyone expected.

## Tone

- Body-horror premise filtered through an arcade-puzzle tone.
- A hostile laboratory setting with dark humor underneath it.
- Mike's power feels grotesque, useful, and empowering at the same time.

## Narrative Direction

The game should follow Mike's escape through a succession of underground facility levels. Each area introduces new dangers, containment systems, and personnel trying to stop him.

Mike does not defeat enemies through direct combat. Instead, he survives, outmaneuvers, and occasionally causes collateral damage by redirecting the facility's own hazards back into the environment or into the people hunting him.

## Core Themes

- Invincibility as transformation rather than invulnerability.
- Escape from institutional cruelty.
- Turning harm into progress.
- Using the enemy's systems against them.
